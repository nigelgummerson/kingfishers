# Kingfishers of the World — Complete Species List

*Compiled 2026-02-17. Taxonomy follows IOC World Bird List v15.1 (February 2025).*

## Taxonomic Overview

Modern taxonomy places all kingfishers in a single family **Alcedinidae** with three subfamilies (some authorities treat these as separate families):

| Subfamily | Common Name | Species | Genera |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Alcedininae** | River kingfishers | ~37 | 4 |
| **Halcyoninae** | Tree kingfishers | ~70 | 11 |
| **Cerylinae** | Water kingfishers | 9 | 3 |

**Total: ~115 species** (exact count varies by authority, 114–119).

### The "57 species" question

Older references from the 1980s–1990s listed roughly 85–90 kingfisher species worldwide. The jump to 114–119 is largely driven by island population splits in *Ceyx* (from ~11 to ~23 species) and *Todiramphus* (from ~20 to ~30 species). The figure of 57 may reflect a count of one subfamily from a particular source, or a narrower species concept current in the early 1990s.

## Conservation Summary

| IUCN Category | Count | Notable Species |
|---|---|---|
| **EW** (Extinct in the Wild) | 1 | Guam Kingfisher |
| **CR** (Critically Endangered) | 4 | Blue-banded, Sangihe Dwarf (possibly extinct), Marquesan, Tuamotu, Niau |
| **EN** (Endangered) | 1 | Kofiau Paradise Kingfisher |
| **VU** (Vulnerable) | ~9 | Philippine Dwarf, Bismarck, Winchell's, Sombre, Mewing, Blue-capped, Moustached, N & S Silvery |
| **NT** (Near Threatened) | ~17 | Including **New Britain Kingfisher** (recommended uplisting to VU) |
| **LC** (Least Concern) | ~85 | Majority of species |

**New Britain hosts 3 kingfisher species:** New Britain Kingfisher (*T. albonotatus*), New Britain Dwarf Kingfisher (*C. sacerdotis*), and Black-capped Paradise Kingfisher (*T. nigriceps*) — all highlighted in bold above.
