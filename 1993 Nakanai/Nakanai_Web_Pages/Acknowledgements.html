Acknowledgements

The members of the Nakanai '93 expedition would like to thank all our sponsors who generously contributed, either in help or in kind, and in doing so made the expedition possible.

In particular we would like to thank John Wijnand of Hargy Oil Plantations, who provided us with constant support, help and encouragement, especially when things were not quite going according to plan. We were all very sorry to hear of his illness, which meant that we were not able to thank him in person.

We also greatly appreciate the hospitality of Alan and Rita Morrow of West New Britain Oil Plantations, who put us up at very short notice, as well as feeding us vast quantities of food!

Our thanks is also extended to Max Henderson, director of the Pacific Heritage Foundation, and Marsha Bermann from the West New Britain Provincial Government. Thanks to their invitations we were able to conduct research in important parts of New Britain.

From the University of Auckland we are very grateful to Professor Euan Young who arranged accommodation and transport for us during our time in New Zealand; and from the University of Papua New Guinea, in Port Moresby, we are indebted to Professor Ian Burrows, Dr Monica Raul and to Paulus Kea, our expedition guide, interpreter and friend.

Our thanks also to Mike Hopkins who was our initial contact at the University of PNG, to Bob Prior of the Cocoa and Coconut Research Institute in Rabaul, who provided us with maps, and to Jane Ramdoyl at the London embassy who ensured that we did, eventually, receive visas.

Finally we would like to thank the patron of our expedition Dr. Bruce Beehler, and our home-agent Dr George McGavin, for their advice and encouragement.
