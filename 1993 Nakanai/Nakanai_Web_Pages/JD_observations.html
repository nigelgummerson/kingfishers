The difference in the number of species at the two sites can be investigated using a sign test. The output was as follows:-

NBelowEqualAboveP-Value
64934210.0428

The P-value is significant at the 95% significance level. From this we can conclude that there is a significant difference in the number of species recorded at the two sites.

The difference in the distribution of species between the diet catagories can be investigated using a G-test (Zar '84).

For each site, each diet catagory contains a proportion of the total number of species at that site. The G-test compares the proportions to see whether the distribution of species between the diet catagories at Talawe is significantly different from that at Wid Dog. The lichen- feeders were omitted from this analysis as their value at Talawe is zero - an invalid value for the G-test. The outcome was as follows:-

G= 4.024 with 7 degrees of freedom.

Looking this up in a chi-square table, we find the value is only significant at the 75% level.

Therefore we cannot reject the possibility that the difference between the two sites in the distribution of species between the diet catagories is due to chance alone.
