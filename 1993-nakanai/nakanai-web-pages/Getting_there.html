Getting There

Pre-Departure

In the last months leading up to the departure date, the status of the expedition was uncertain. Funding was the biggest headache, as the expedition budget was approximately Ł10,000. Despite generous funding from grant-giving bodies, support from the private sector was very limited. Only a last minute donation and parental loans enabled us to buy flights. The last two weeks of June were a blur of equipment purchasing and travel organisation. Visas still had not arrived by our departure date but it was decided to travel anyway as we had a week in New Zealand to sort out the mess. Grey-haired and fatigued, we left the UK on the 27th of June for three months of education.

New Zealand

Our time in Auckland was immensely enjoyable, mainly thanks to Professor Euan Young and all at the University of Auckland. We stayed at the university field station in Leigh, where we slept, feasted and tried out our equipment for five days. Hectic phone calling and pleading also resulted in the arrival of our visas, much to everyone's relief. After this, we moved to Auckland city for the weekend before our flight. The small area of New Zealand we saw was very beautiful and this, combined with the hospitality of the people, made us sorry to leave.

Port Moresby

Port Moresby, the capital of PNG, is a place with an unpleasant reputation and we were naturally hoping to keep our stay there short. As it turned out we achieved much during our week-long stay and Dr. Ian Burrows and all at the University of PNG made us very welcome. It was here that we met Paulus Kea, a technician in the Biology Department, who was to spend five weeks with us on New Britain, collecting insects and vertebrates from the little-known New Britain fauna. Paulus was an invaluable member of our team without whom we could never have achieved as much as we did.

With flights and accommodation arranged, more basic equipment acquired and a brief taste of things to come (after a day birding in the Brown River forest), we left Port Moresby on the 14th of July, feeling like intrepid explorers journeying into the unknown!
