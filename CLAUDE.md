# Kingfishers of the World

## What This Project Is
A personal research project compiling data on all the world's kingfisher species (~115 spp., family Alcedinidae), with a particular focus on the **New Britain Kingfisher** (*Todiramphus albonotatus*). Nigel visited New Britain in 1993 as part of the **Nakanai '93** Oxford University expedition to study the population of this species and undertake other ornithological and zoological fieldwork.

## Key Facts
- **Total kingfisher species (IOC 2025):** ~115 (3 subfamilies, 18 genera)
- **New Britain Kingfisher:** *Todiramphus albonotatus* (formerly *Halcyon albonotata*)
- **IUCN status:** Near Threatened (recommended uplisting to Vulnerable)
- **Population:** 2,500–10,000 mature individuals (estimated)
- **Primary threat:** Oil palm plantation expansion in lowland New Britain
- **New Britain hosts 3 kingfisher species:** T. albonotatus, Ceyx sacerdotis, Tanysiptera nigriceps

## Nakanai '93 Expedition
- **Oxford University expedition, 27 Jun – 16 Sep 1993** (12 weeks) — Future Conservationist Award (BP Conservation Programme, now CLP)
- **Team:** Jonathan Clay (leader), Nigel Gummerson, Nick Mason, Jamie (surname unknown), Paulus (PNG guide)
- **Sites:** Lake Hargy (25–31 Jul), Wild Dog/Bainings (4–13 Aug), Hornbill Point (21–26 Aug), Mount Talawe (27 Aug – 3 Sep)
- **Original plan** (montane Nakanai avifauna survey) abandoned due to access problems; redirected to threatened sites
- **Partners:** Pacific Heritage Foundation, WNBPG (Marsha Bermann), Hargy Oil Palm (John Wijnand)
- **7 kingfisher species recorded** — White-mantled (2 pairs: Wild Dog + Talawe), Bismarck's, Dwarf, Black-headed Paradise, Sacred, Collared, River
- **Sound recordings deposited with BLOWS** — NB Fruit Dove confirmed; kingfisher status unknown
- **Expedition report** not published online; not cited in subsequent NB bird literature
- **Historical events:** Witnessed Langila eruption from Hornbill Point (Aug 1993); MV Kris sank ~2 days after team disembarked (5+ lives lost, operator Peter Sharp later charged over MV Rabaul Queen disaster 2012)
- **CLP page:** https://www.conservationleadershipprogramme.org/project/nakanai-93-oxford-university-expedition-new-britain-island-papua-new-guinea/

## Notes
- The species was known as *Halcyon albonotata* at the time of the 1993 fieldwork
- IOC uses the common name "White-mantled Kingfisher"; eBird/Birds of the World uses "New Britain Kingfisher"
- Breeding biology, diet, and detailed ecology remain almost entirely unknown — significant research gaps
- Only 5 foreground sound recordings exist on Xeno-canto (mostly Frank Lambert, 2007–2009)
- Very few photographs in public databases
- The Nakanai '93 data predates the major oil palm expansion period — potentially valuable baseline
